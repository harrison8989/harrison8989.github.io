History

One of the longest-cultivated plants, beans have been eaten in cultures around the globe, ranging from Afghanistan to Thailand to Egypt. An important source of protein, many cultures still eat beans today, in both the Old and New Worlds.

Beans were an important part of people's diets. As an example, pre-Columbian people often used the "Three Sisters" method of growing companion plants, which involved beans, maize, and squash.

There are about 40,000 different varieties of beans, while only a fraction are mass-produced for consumption.
