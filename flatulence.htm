Flatulence

In medical literature, "flatulence" is described as "flatus expelled through the anus" or the "quality or state of being flatulent". The scientific study of flatulence is termed flatology, while scientists who study flatology are termed flatologists.

Flatulence usual occurs when flatus passes through the rectum, although the quantity and frequence can vary greatly between individuals. The intestinal gas may also have a feculent smell, although this also varies greatly between individuals.

Eating beans causes flatulence due to the presence of oligosaccharides in beans, and a certain enzyme is necessary to digest these molecules. Humans do not naturally produce these enzymes, and so bacteria process these enzymes instead, with the result of creating flatulence-causing gases. There are ways of diminishing the effects of flatulence, such as by draining the water in which beans have been cooked or soaking the beans in baking soda water overnight.
